# NimbusCRM — Champion Transition Playbook

## Overview
This playbook outlines the process for managing customer relationships when a key champion or executive sponsor departs from the customer organization. Champion departure is one of the highest-risk events in the customer lifecycle.

## Definition: Champion
A champion is the internal advocate inside the customer's organization who drives adoption, secures budget, and sponsors renewal decisions. Losing a champion without a replacement relationship creates significant churn and non-renewal risk.

## Risk Level Matrix

| Champion Tenure | Days to Renewal | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|
| < 6 months | Any | HIGH |
| Any | < 60 days | CRITICAL |
| > 12 months | < 90 days | HIGH |
| Any | > 120 days | MEDIUM |

## Phase 1: Immediate Response (Days 1–7)

### Actions
1. **Acknowledge the departure** — Send a warm, human response to any point of contact at the customer acknowledging the transition.
2. **Identify the successor** — Ask remaining contacts who is taking over the departed champion's responsibilities.
3. **Do not send renewal or commercial emails** during the first 7 days — relationship stability comes first.
4. **Internal alert** — Flag the account as "champion at risk" in CRM and alert CSM manager.

## Phase 2: New Stakeholder Engagement (Days 7–21)

### Actions
1. **Introductory call** — Schedule a no-agenda, relationship-building intro call with the new contact within 14 days.
2. **Platform value summary** — Prepare a concise (1-page) executive summary of what NimbusCRM has delivered for the account to date (metrics, workflows, ROI).
3. **Do not assume continuity** — Prior commitments (verbal renewals, expansion plans) made by the departed champion are NOT binding. Treat the new stakeholder as a fresh conversation.
4. **Understand new stakeholder priorities** — Ask about their goals, not the prior champion's goals.

## Phase 3: Relationship Re-Establishment (Days 21–60)

### Actions
1. **Executive alignment call** — If the new contact is VP-level or above, involve your own CSM leadership.
2. **Customized success plan** — Rebuild the joint success plan around the new stakeholder's priorities.
3. **Renewal discussion** — Only initiate renewal conversation after a positive relationship signal from the new contact (e.g., a completed product review or success call).
4. **Offer a transition incentive** — Consider offering a 30-day extension or a complimentary onboarding workshop to build goodwill.

## Warning Signs (Escalate Immediately)
- New stakeholder has not responded to 2+ outreach attempts
- New stakeholder is evaluating competitor platforms
- Renewal is less than 30 days away with no relationship established
- Legal team or procurement involved without prior notice

## Escalation Protocol
If any warning signs are present, escalate to VP of Customer Success within 24 hours. Do not attempt to close a renewal during an unresolved champion transition without leadership involvement.
