# NimbusCRM — Pricing & Upgrade Guide

**Document Type:** Internal Pricing Reference  
**Audience:** Customer Success Managers, Account Executives  
**Effective Date:** January 2026  

---

## Subscription Plans

### Starter Plan — $299/month (up to 10 seats)
- Core CRM (contacts, accounts, deals)
- Basic pipeline management
- Standard reporting
- Email integration
- Community support

---

### Business Plan — $799/month base + $65/seat/month
- Everything in Starter
- Advanced automation workflows
- Custom dashboards
- API access (read/write)
- JIRA and Slack integrations
- Priority support (48hr response)
- Dedicated CSM (above 50 seats)

**Most Common Plan — 60% of mid-market customers**

---

### Enterprise Plan — Custom pricing (starting $10k/month)
- Everything in Business
- Unlimited seats
- AI Insights Dashboard
- Advanced analytics and forecasting
- SSO (SAML 2.0)
- SOC 2 Type II compliance documentation
- Custom integrations and webhooks
- SLA guarantee (99.9% uptime)
- Dedicated Customer Success team
- Quarterly Business Reviews included

---

## Upgrade Triggers (When to Propose Upsell)

| Signal | Recommended Action |
|---|---|
| User count approaching plan seat limit | Propose seat expansion or Enterprise upgrade |
| Customer asking about AI features | Demo Enterprise AI Insights Dashboard |
| Customer asking about SSO | Enterprise required — major upgrade trigger |
| APAC/multi-region expansion | Enterprise includes regional CSM support |
| Customer citing "reporting limitations" | Business plan custom dashboards or Enterprise |
| >100 users on Business plan | Cost-efficiency often favors Enterprise flat rate |

## Discount Policy

**Standard Discounts (CSM Authority):**
- 2-year commitment: 10% annual discount
- 3-year commitment: 18% annual discount
- Non-profit: 20% discount (requires verification)

**Requires VP Approval:**
- Discounts >20%
- Custom pricing below minimum thresholds
- Competitor match (must document competitive situation)

---

## Expansion Playbook

**Step 1:** Identify expansion signal (new department, new region, new use case)  
**Step 2:** Document current value delivered (usage metrics, ROI data)  
**Step 3:** Present expansion ROI (cost per seat improvement, productivity gain)  
**Step 4:** Offer multi-year discount to lock in commitment  
**Step 5:** Involve VP of Sales if deal value >$100k incremental  

---

## Competitive Positioning

**vs. Salesforce:**  
NimbusCRM wins on simplicity, implementation speed (days vs. months), and TCO for mid-market companies. Position against Salesforce when customer mentions "too complex" or "too expensive."

**vs. HubSpot:**  
NimbusCRM wins on B2B deal pipeline depth and enterprise integrations. HubSpot is stronger for B2C and marketing-led growth.
